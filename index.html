3D Model Critical Discourse Analysis on EMI Programs’ Promotion in Universities' International Program and International Schools in Indonesia ABSTRACT

EMI implementation in Indonesia is often different from the promotion. This study aims to find the ideology, strategy, and the reality of EMI promotions and implementations in universities' international programs and international schools in Indonesia. This study is conducted using Fairclough's (1995) 3D Models CDA to see the promotions of EMI programs in three levels: textual, discursive, and sociocultural. Sociological concepts are used to see the nature of ideology behind EMI promotion, and Kachru's (1992) World Englishes theory is used to connect the finding with Indonesian context as a part of Expanding Circle. Interviews of 2 students are further added to expand the analysis. This study found that EMI programs in Indonesian universities' promotional strategies employ both symbolic and social capital (Bourdieu, 1986), Assumed Knowledge (Fairclough, 1995) to condition class reproduction and stand as an institutional authority. On the other hand, Indonesian international schools approach a more market-driven strategies with Attention Economy by Davenport and Beck (2001), Pastoral Power (Foucault, 1982). Both the schools and the universities show a sign of English hegemony behind the implementation of EMI programs in Indonesia. The findings suggest that assessments of EMI programs are needed, considering the fact that they act more as a symbolic capital, social capital, and marketing strategy, rather than a pedagogical approach.
